Making Modes More Engaging

You've chosen to refine the separate Sakura and Momiji modes. Here are 3 premium ways to make them "Pop".

1

The "Dynamic Aura"

Visuals: Soft-blurred, pulsing auras in the background that follow the mouse. High-contrast typography with dramatic reveal animations.

  • Highly immersive and atmospheric
  • Feels premium and "alive"
  • Heavier on GPU (but optimized)
2

The "Bento Glass"

Visuals: A structured Bento grid layout with interactive "glass" cards. Each theme (Sakura/Momiji) gets unique glass textures and border glows.

  • Very organized and information-dense
  • Extremely modern "Apple-like" aesthetic
  • More complex to implement responsively
3

The "Generative Nature"

Visuals: Uses organic, generative paths that react to the mode (Cherry blossoms vs. Autumn winds). Uses multi-layered parallax effects for deep spatial depth.

  • Unique and artistic
  • Strong connection to the "Sakura/Momiji" names
  • Can be visually busy if not balanced

My Recommendation

Approach 2: The "Bento Glass"

This is the most "attractive" and "professional" choice. It allows us to keep the separate modes distinct but gives the hero section a high-end, structured look that feels like a production-grade application. We can use Approach 1's "Aura" as a subtle background effect behind the bento cards.
